How It Works
The Nest is Yolk Brands' central marketing operations platform — SEO, social, content, competitor intelligence, and team task management in one place. Here's exactly what each piece does.

⚙️ Weekly Automation — Monday 8am Dubai

🗺️ The Full System

📅 Content Calendar — social media operations platform
Plan, review, approve and schedule social content for all 5 brands across all markets. Post types: Static, Carousel, Reel, Story, Copy Only. Full approval workflow — submit → Slack ping to approvers → approve or request changes → export CSV for SocialPilot. Reels and Stories show manual posting instructions with date, time, and video link. AI Caption Generator inside the post form: describe what the post is about or upload images — Claude looks at them and writes a caption + hashtags. Bulk actions: select multiple posts to reschedule, copy to another market, or submit all at once.
📋 Approvals Queue — SEO content to-do list
Every Monday the system analyses GSC data and generates SEO content: meta rewrites, page updates, blog drafts, new pages. Each item shows: keyword, current ranking, expected impact (estimated extra clicks/month if approved), and brand voice score. Items scoring 5–7 on voice are automatically rewritten before they reach you — only genuinely borderline content needs your attention. Click Approve → WP Draft to push to WordPress, or Publish Live to go live immediately. Rewrite with AI sends it back with specific feedback. Select multiple items with checkboxes to bulk dismiss.
📊 Analytics & ROI — intelligence hub
Five views in one tab: GSC Rankings (your real Google data), Competitor Matrix (weekly SERP data with Share of Voice, Gaps, and auto-discovery of unknown competitors), Competitor Analysis (deep audit of any domain — their keywords, PageSpeed, on-page signals, vs your positions — with AI-generated recommended actions routing to queue/Perch/dev kanban), Backlinks, and Keyword Opportunities (scored list of gaps Claude filters for menu relevance — queue for AI or assign to team via Perch). All views support market filtering — switch to Bahrain to see Bahrain-specific data.
📈 Reports — executive scorecard
CEO-ready weekly view: top 10 keywords, traffic value in AED (real CPC data), position distribution, AI Readiness Score (7 checks), competitor gap summary (narrative — "Salt owns 12 keywords you don't rank for"), keyword opportunities summary, AI Search Presence (Google AI Overviews + ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini/Claude mentions in one card), GA4 website traffic, and Goals & Progress (set targets in Settings — progress bars show where you stand vs Q4 targets). Export as PDF for board presentations.
⚡ Technical SEO — site health + developer kanban
Weekly automated audit: Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS, TBT), mobile/desktop PageSpeed, canonical errors, robots.txt, sitemap. Issues auto-create tasks in the Developer Kanban — your external developer picks these up from a dedicated board. A mobile score below 70/100 blocks Google AI Overview eligibility. International market health checked separately.
📍 Local SEO — GBP + citations
GBP location health cards for all Pickl and Bonbird locations. Citation tracker checks your listings on Zomato, TripAdvisor, Time Out Dubai, What's On, and The Entertainer weekly — flag issues or mark verified. Review reply drafting in brand voice (GBP API activation pending). GBP is the #1 local pack signal — a complete, active profile is required for map results.
🌍 International SEO — Market Hub
Covers all 6 Pickl markets (UAE, Bahrain, KSA, Qatar, Egypt, Jordan, Oman) and 3 Bonbird markets (Oman, Pakistan, Qatar). Each market card shows: Top 10 rankings for that market, keyword opportunities count, items queued. One click opens keyword opportunities or competitor analysis pre-filled for that market — no manual switching. Keyword discovery runs per market every Monday using market-specific location codes and seed keywords, not generic UAE data. Claude understands URL structure (/bh/ = Bahrain, /ksa/ = Saudi Arabia) and writes for the specific market audience.
✨ AI Content Studio — on-demand content tools
Run any time: Review Responder, Schema Generator, Content Brief Builder, On-Demand Page Audit, YouTube SEO Brief Generator (titles, description, tags, VideoObject schema, content outline). All tools pre-loaded with brand context and real writing examples — output sounds like Pickl or Bonbird, not generic AI.
🪺 The Perch — team task management
Trello replacement for the marketing team. Drag-drop kanban with labels, due dates, comments, Slack notifications. Filters: brand, department, label, assignee, priority, text search, overdue toggle, sort by due date or priority. The Action Engine routes strategic tasks here automatically — competitor gaps, keyword opportunities, and AI-recommended actions all land in The Perch when they need human judgment rather than automated execution. Daily 9am Dubai digest shows overdue tasks and what's due today.

🎯 Keyword Tiers — How Keywords Are Prioritised

Every keyword in your queue is classified by how close it is to ranking. The tier tells you the expected effort and timeline.

⚡
Quick Win Position 11–20
You already rank on page 2. One strong content update pushes you to page 1. Fastest ROI — expect results in days to weeks.
📈
Short Term Position 21–35
Ranking weakly. A focused new post or page rewrite builds relevance. Expect results in 4–8 weeks.
🎯
Long Term Position 36–100
Barely visible. New content from scratch builds authority over time. Expect results in 2–4 months.
🚨
Priority Gap Seed list — not in GSC yet
Keywords you've manually added to the Seed list that you want to own. No impressions yet — earliest stage. Highest strategic value.

📋 Approval Types — What Each Item Means

META UPDATE Rewrites the SEO title and meta description for an existing page. Low risk, high CTR impact. Triggered when a page has high impressions but below-expected click-through rate. Always approve these.
PAGE UPDATE Rewrites the full content of an existing page to push a Quick Win keyword to page 1. Review content before approving. Check the brand voice score — aim for 7+.
BLOG DRAFT A brand new blog post targeting a content gap keyword. Drafted in brand voice, voice-checked, then queued. Check the voice score badge — aim for 7+ before publishing.
NEW PAGE A new landing page for a location or service keyword (e.g. "burger delivery abu dhabi"). The developer sets up the WordPress page, then approves here to publish content.
REVIEW REPLY A suggested response to a Google review — drafted in brand voice. Always personalise with the customer's name or specific comment before publishing.

✦ Brand Voice Scoring

Every blog draft and page update is scored 1–10 for brand voice accuracy before it reaches your queue. Claude checks for generic phrases, banned words, AI tells like em dashes, and whether the content sounds specifically like Pickl or Bonbird. Items scoring 5–7 are automatically rewritten targeting the specific issues before queuing — so what you see has already had one auto-fix pass. Only content that still has voice issues after the auto-fix shows a warning badge.

9–10 On Brand Sharp, specific, unmistakably the brand. Approve with confidence.
7–8 On Brand Mostly strong. Minor generic moments. Fine to approve.
5–6 Review Voice Some brand voice but generic in places. Use Rewrite with AI or edit manually.
< 5 Auto-rejected Scored below 5 — never queued. You'll see an error in Settings & Logs if you run the scheduler manually.

🤖 AI Search Presence — Why This Matters

Every Monday, The Nest asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini "What are the best burger restaurants in Dubai?" (and 5 other category queries per brand). It records whether your brand is mentioned and builds a weekly trend.

Why LLMs matter: Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Gemini are increasingly used for restaurant recommendations — especially by tourists and expats using AI assistants. Not appearing means losing customers before they ever search Google.
How to improve it: LLMs train on web content. More blog posts, press mentions, review platforms, and structured data (schema) = more LLM visibility. Content approved through this tool directly contributes to LLM mention rates over time.

⚔️ Competitor Matrix — How to Use It

The matrix refreshes every Monday from DataForSEO (real Google SERP data, UAE desktop). Here's how each view is useful:

Rankings: Side-by-side position comparison for every tracked keyword. SERP feature pills show who owns featured snippets and local pack. Movement arrows show week-on-week trends. Unknown competitor alerts surface brands appearing in your SERPs that you're not tracking yet.
Share of Voice: Who captures the most estimated clicks across your keyword category. Calculated using real CTR curves (position 1 = 30%, position 5 = 7%, etc.). More meaningful than simple rank counts. Shows the 12-week trend line so you can see if you're gaining or losing market share.
Gaps: Keywords where competitors rank top 20 but you don't. "No gaps" means you rank for everything tracked — not that there are no opportunities. Expand the tracking list in Manage Keywords to discover more. Each gap is a direct content brief: create the page, target the keyword, close the gap.
Keyword strategy tip: The more keywords you track, the more gaps and Share of Voice data you get. Aim for 100+ tracked keywords per brand covering: head terms, location modifiers, intent variants (delivery, near me, best), and format variants (burger, wrap, chicken).

🚨 Priority Gap Keywords (Seed List)

Keywords you want to rank for that don't appear in GSC yet. The scheduler targets these alongside GSC data every Monday. Add anything a competitor ranks for that you don't — these become the highest-priority content briefs.
